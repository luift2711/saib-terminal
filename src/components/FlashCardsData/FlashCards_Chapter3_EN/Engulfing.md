# Flashcards: Engulfing

Q: What is a Bullish Engulfing?
A: A two-candlestick pattern: A red candle followed by a green candle. The green candle's body is larger and completely "engulfs" the red candle's body. A strong bullish reversal signal at the end of a downtrend.

Q: What is a Bearish Engulfing?
A: The opposite: A green candle followed by a red candle that completely engulfs the green candle. The Sellers (Bears) suddenly take over the entire territory of the Buyers (Bulls). A bearish reversal signal at the end of an uptrend.

Q: What if an Engulfing pattern appears in the middle of a trend (before reaching Support/Resistance)?
A: It has no reversal value. This candlestick pattern is only strong when it appears at a significant level (S/R, Fibo 62-79%).
