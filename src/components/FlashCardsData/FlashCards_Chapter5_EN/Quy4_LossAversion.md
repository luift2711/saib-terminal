# Flashcards: Demon 4 - Loss Aversion

Q: What are the consequences of Loss Aversion?
A: Fearing the pain of cutting losses, a trader moves the Stop Loss further away or removes it entirely to hold onto losers in hopes that "price will come back". Turns a small loss into an irrecoverable one.

Q: What are the symptoms of Loss Aversion?
A: Moving the Stop Loss when the price gets close. Closing winning trades very early but holding losing trades very long. Feeling relieved when holding a loss back to break-even instead of recognizing the mistake.

Q: What is the Loss Aversion Exorcism Ritual?
A: Pre-mortem. Accept the SL amount as a "business expense" BEFORE entering the trade. Once the SL is set, absolutely never move it further away.
