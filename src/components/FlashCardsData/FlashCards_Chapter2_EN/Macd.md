# Flashcards: MACD (Measuring Momentum)

Q: What are the components of MACD?
A: 1. MACD Line (Fast Trend Line)
2. Signal Line (Smoothed Signal Line)
3. Histogram (Bar chart showing the difference between the two lines).

Q: When does a MACD Golden Cross occur?
A: When the MACD Line crosses ABOVE the Signal Line. Signals momentum shifting to an Upward (Bullish) direction.

Q: What is the meaning of the Histogram in MACD?
A: When the green bars get progressively higher: Upward momentum is strengthening.
When the red bars get progressively deeper: Downward momentum is strengthening.
Bars change from red to green (crossing the 0 line): Phase reversal signal.

Q: What is MACD Divergence?
A: Similar to RSI. When price makes a higher high but the MACD Histogram's peak is lower → Warns that upward momentum is exhausted, about to reverse.
