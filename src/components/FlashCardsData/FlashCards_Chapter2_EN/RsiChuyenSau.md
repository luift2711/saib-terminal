# Flashcards: RSI — In-depth

Q: What is the RSI (Relative Strength Index) used for?
A: To measure the speed and change of price movements to evaluate "Overbought" or "Oversold" conditions.

Q: What do RSI > 70 and RSI < 30 mean?
A: RSI > 70: Overbought zone (Fever), a downward correction may occur.
RSI < 30: Oversold zone (Hypothermia), an upward recovery may occur.
(Note: RSI > 70 does NOT mean you must sell immediately).

Q: What is RSI Divergence?
A: It is the strongest RSI signal, occurring when price moves in the opposite direction of the RSI.
Bearish Divergence: Price makes a HIGHER high, but RSI makes a LOWER high. Warns of a bearish reversal.

Q: What is the most common mistake when using RSI?
A: Selling immediately when RSI > 70. In a strong uptrend, RSI can remain > 80 for many consecutive weeks.
