# Flashcards: Leverage & Margin

Q: What is Leverage?
A: A tool to borrow capital from the exchange to trade a larger volume than the amount of money you have. It helps amplify profits but also amplifies the risk of losses.

Q: What is Margin?
A: The mandatory "deposit" amount required to open and maintain a position when using leverage.

Q: What is a Margin Call / Stop Out?
A: Occurs when the amount of loss exceeds the Margin (or reaches the exchange's specified threshold). The exchange will automatically liquidate (close) your position to prevent losses from encroaching on the exchange's funds.

Q: What is the biggest danger of high Leverage?
A: If the price moves against you by a very small amount (e.g., 1%), you could lose your entire account capital if you trade "full margin".
