# Flashcards: Reading Japanese Candlesticks

Q: What are the components of a Japanese candlestick?
A: It consists of 4 price levels: Open, Close, High, and Low. The distance between Open and Close forms the Body. The Wick/Shadow indicates price rejection.

Q: What are the characteristics of a Bullish Candle?
A: The closing price (Close) is higher than the opening price (Open). It is usually green. It indicates that buyers (Bulls) are in control of the trading session.

Q: What are the characteristics of a Bearish Candle?
A: The closing price (Close) is lower than the opening price (Open). It is usually red. It indicates that sellers (Bears) are in control of the trading session.

Q: What does a Doji candle indicate?
A: The candle has an opening price and closing price that are nearly equal. It indicates indecision, a balance between buyers and sellers.

Q: What does a Marubozu candle indicate?
A: The candle has a very long body and extremely short wicks. It indicates extremely strong and dominant momentum from one side (Buyers or Sellers).
