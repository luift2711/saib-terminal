# Flashcards: Core Terminology

Q: What do Bull and Bear represent?
A: Bull = Buyers (Expect prices to rise). Bear = Sellers (Expect prices to fall).

Q: What does Buy / Long mean?
A: To open a buy order. You profit when the price increases.

Q: What does Sell / Short mean?
A: To open a short sell order. You profit when the price decreases (Borrow and sell first at a high price, buy back to return at a low price).

Q: What is a PIP?
A: The smallest unit of price change (usually at the fourth decimal place). Example: EUR/USD moving from 1.1000 to 1.1005 is a 5 pip increase.

Q: What is Spread?
A: The difference between the Ask price and the Bid price. This is the "fee" you pay to the broker when opening an order.

Q: What are Stop Loss (SL) and Take Profit (TP)?
A: SL (Stop Loss): An automatic order closure price to limit losses. TP (Take Profit): An automatic order closure price when the expected profit is reached.
