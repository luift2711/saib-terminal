# Flashcards: Timeframes

Q: What is a Timeframe?
A: It is the period required to form a single candlestick. For example: An H1 timeframe means each candlestick represents price movement within 1 hour. A D1 timeframe is 1 day.

Q: Why do candlesticks look different on different timeframes?
A: Because they aggregate different data. One H1 candlestick is formed from 4 M15 candlesticks, or 12 M5 candlesticks.

Q: What is the relationship between timeframes?
A: Larger timeframes (D1, H4) provide the main trend and higher reliability. Smaller timeframes (M15, M5) provide detailed price action to find better entry points.

Q: What is the "Multi-timeframe" principle?
A: Always analyze from larger timeframes to identify the main trend, then zoom into smaller timeframes to find trading points.
