# Flashcards: 1-2% Rule & Position Sizing

Q: What does the 1-2% Rule mean?
A: Each trade only accepts a maximum risk of losing 1% to 2% of the total capital. If your account has 100 candies, each time you play you only bet 1-2 candies. Helps preserve capital even when facing a losing streak.

Q: What is the formula for calculating Position Size?
A: Trade volume (Size) = Risk amount ($) / (Stop Loss pips × Value of 1 pip).

Q: How does Risk % have a non-linear effect?
A: If risking 5%/trade, 8 consecutive losses will wipe out 33% of the account. If risking 1%/trade, 8 consecutive losses will only lose about 7.7% of the account.
