# Flashcards: Pyramiding & Scaling

Q: What is correct Pyramiding (Standard Pyramid)?
A: Adding to a winning position in the direction of the trend: The first trade has the largest volume (Base). Subsequent trades have gradually smaller volumes (usually ≤ 50% of the previous trade). Total risk does not increase.

Q: How dangerous is incorrect Pyramiding (Inverted Pyramid)?
A: Adding to positions with increasing volume (e.g., trade 1 is 1 lot, trade 2 is 2 lots, trade 3 is 4 lots). A single reversal will cause the largest trade at the top to wipe out all profits.

Q: What is the prerequisite before Pyramiding?
A: ONLY add new trades when the original trade has its Stop Loss moved to Break Even.
