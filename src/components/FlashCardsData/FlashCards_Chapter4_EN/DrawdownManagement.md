# Flashcards: Drawdown Management

Q: What is a Drawdown?
A: The percentage decline of an account from its most recent peak down to its current lowest point. A large drawdown is the number 1 enemy of an account.

Q: When is the Circuit Breaker rule applied?
A: - Loss of 3-5% in 1 day: Stop trading for that day.
- Loss of 7-10% in 1 week: Reduce position size by 50% for the following week.
- Loss of 15-20% in 1 month: Stop trading completely for 2 weeks to review the system.

Q: What to do to recover from a large Drawdown?
A: Take a break to reset your psychology. Review your trading journal without emotion (only note rule-breaking trades). When returning, trade with a small size (50%) until stabilized.
