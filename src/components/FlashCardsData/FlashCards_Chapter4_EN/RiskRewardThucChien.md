# Flashcards: Practical Risk:Reward

Q: What is the Risk:Reward (R:R) ratio?
A: It is the ratio between the amount you can lose (Risk) and the amount you can make (Reward) on 1 trade. R:R 1:3 means risking 1 unit to expect a profit of 3 units.

Q: Why does a good R:R mean you don't need a high Win Rate?
A: For example, with R:R = 1:2, you only need to win >33.3% of trades to break even. Winning 40% means consistent profits.

Q: What are smart trade exit techniques?
A: - Partial Exit: Take profit on a portion (e.g., 50%) at TP1, let the rest run to TP2.
- Move to Break Even: Once a trade is 1R in profit, move the SL to the Entry point to ensure a risk-free trade.
